# Intervention Protocol: Drive-Fun

## Program Structure

**Number of participants per group:** 3–6 adolescents  

**Number of sessions:**  
7 individual intervention sessions and 4 group intervention sessions  
**Total:** 11 sessions  

**Duration of group sessions:** 90 minutes  
**Duration of individual sessions:** 60 minutes  

---

## Table A: Summary of Treatment Units

### Preparation Phase – Session 1

**Goals**  
a. Distribution of intervention-related forms to adolescents prior to the beginning of the intervention  
b. Creation of a shared electronic repository for the adolescents  
c. Initial acquaintance with adolescents and their parents  

**Methods**  
Mailing a personal folder containing all intervention materials to each adolescent  
Sending a link to a shared Google Drive folder  
Parent interview and occupational profile interview with the adolescent (OPHI)  

**Format**  
One session conducted in the laboratory with parental presence  

---

### Unit A – Development of Adaptive Self-Awareness (Sessions 2–5)

**Goals**  
a. Identification of personal strengths in the context of driving  
b. Understanding individual challenges stemming from ADHD and executive function deficits and their implications for driving  
c. Initial acquisition of monitoring and reflective abilities  

**Methods**  
Guided learning using informational resources  
Experiential activities including games and cognitive tasks  
Reflection on occupational experiences and simulator driving performance  

**Format**  
Three group sessions conducted in the laboratory and one remote group session  

---

### Unit B – Acquisition of General and Driving-Specific Strategies (Sessions 6–9)

**Goals**  
a. Acquisition of general executive strategies: goal setting, task demand analysis, planning, and monitoring  
b. Acquisition of driving-specific strategies tailored to the adolescent’s driving profile  
c. Initial development of self-advocacy skills  

**Methods**  
Analysis of the occupational profile  
Guided discovery and strategy learning linked to personal goals  
Practice and application of strategies across contexts, including simulator driving  

**Format**  
Three individual sessions in the laboratory and one remote individual session  

---

### Unit C – Summary and Consolidation of the Therapeutapeutic Process (Sessions 10–11)

**Goals**  
a. Review of the therapeutic process and its outcomes  
b. Peer sharing of personal experiences  
c. Parent meeting and presentation of the personal project  

**Methods**  
Preparation of a creative final project presenting the personal profile, achieved goals, and acquired strategies, and illustrating how the process relates to driving skills  
Presentation of the project in a joint meeting with parents  

**Format**  
One group session in the laboratory and one individual session with parental presence  

---

## Detailed Description of the Intervention

### Preparation Phase

Each adolescent receives a personal folder by mail containing all materials used during the intervention.  
Each adolescent receives access to a shared Google Drive folder.  
**Session 1:** The adolescent and one or both parents meet with the therapist via Zoom for an introductory meeting.  

---

## Unit A – Development of Adaptive Self-Awareness

Group introduction and exploration of personal strengths through feedback from simulator performance, with examples illustrating how strengths support safe driving.  

Psychoeducation regarding ADHD, discussion of personal symptoms, and linkage to driving.  

Psychoeducation regarding individual executive function profiles and their daily-life and driving manifestations.  

**Individual summary session for Unit A:** Occupational Performance Experience Analysis (OPEA) focused on simulator driving.  

---

### Session 2 – Group Session: Identification of Personal Driving Strengths

Introductions and sharing personal motivations for driving.  

Focus on strengths as a foundation for coping with complex driving tasks.  

Completion of a personal strengths worksheet.  

Presentation of concrete driving-related strength examples.  

Cognitive challenge activities to identify thinking styles and strengths.  

Therapist-led reflection and documentation.  

Session summary and personal reflection.  

---

### Session 3 – Group Session: Psychoeducation on ADHD and Its Impact on Driving

Viewing psychoeducational ADHD videos.  

Discussion of personal symptoms and simulator behaviors.  

Completion of the “My ADHD” worksheet.  

Comparison of driving with and without ADHD.  

Discussion of driving complexity and symptom impact.  

Session summary and reflection.  

---

### Session 4 – Group Session: Executive Functions and Driving

Psychoeducation on executive functions based on Brown’s model (2009).  

Review of executive function profiles and BRIEF-SR data when applicable.  

Identification of executive function deficits through unsafe driving videos.  

Discussion of executive functions in safe driving.  

Session summary and reflection.  

---

### Session 5 – Individual Session: Occupational Performance Experience Analysis (OPEA)

Analysis of a successful occupational or simulator driving experience.  

Verbal and written reconstruction of the event.  

Guided analysis using OPEA questions.  

Identification of strategies and executive challenges.  

Development of small, achievable goals.  

---

## Unit B – Acquisition of Strategies and Functional Skills

### Session 6 – Individual: Functional Goal-Setting Strategy (SMART)

Simulator practice.  

Definition and refinement of SMART driving goals.  

COPM rating.  

Documentation in the personal folder.  

---

### Session 7 – Individual: Functional Task Demand Analysis Strategy

Application of task demand analysis to a selected goal.  

Identification of enabling and limiting factors.  

Simulator practice and therapist feedback.  

Session summary.  

---

### Session 8 – Individual: Goal–Plan–Do–Check Strategy

Simulator-based distraction training.  

Guided discovery of the Goal–Plan–Do–Check strategy.  

Selection and practice of personalized strategies.  

Reflection and preparation for subsequent OPEA.  

Session summary.  

---

### Session 9 – Individual: Executive Strategies, Functional Skills, and Self-Advocacy

Development and practice of individualized executive strategies.  

Functional skill acquisition (e.g., planner use).  

Self-advocacy psychoeducation and role-play.  

Simulator-based distraction training.  

Session summary.  

---

## Unit C – Summary, Transfer, and Generalization

### Session 10 – Group Summary Session

Celebration and sharing of personal learning outcomes.  

Development and presentation of a creative final project.  

Group reflection and closure.  

---

### Session 11 – Individual Session with Parents

Presentation of acquired knowledge and skills by the adolescent.  

Positive parental feedback facilitated by the therapist.  

Therapist feedback.  

Completion of a satisfaction questionnaire.
